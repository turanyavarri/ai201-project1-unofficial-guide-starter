It's not that bad all things considered. It's definitely not restaurant quality or even as good as a homemade meal if you can cook but not the prison slop some folks make it out to be. One thing to keep in mind is that there are a lot of different places that accept swipes.

There's the Atrium on CA which is basically just a food court, they're decent but always packed during lunch and dindin.

Livi Student center has a Sbarro's in it that accepts swipes (has a weekend deal, 2 swipes for a full pie and a 2 liter). They're pretty good.

Henry's Diner is usually packed but well worth the wait. Traditional sit down diner on Livi, right across from the first bus stop. (Definitely biased having basically grown up eating diner food) But they're probably the best place to eat if you're fine with waiting or get there early. They stop accepting swipes around 5 and close at 6-7.

Kilmer's market is basically just a minimart/convenience store w/ a "deli" (they sell egg snadwiches, pre-made deli stuff the rest of the day, etc.) they got things like eggs, milk, candy, etc. all for swipes. Right next door to Henry's.

Pretty much the only place of note if you don't have classes on Busch is the dining hall. It's ok, but can be hit or miss. Some days the food is really good, others it's just ok, and sometimes they find a way to fuck up the salad bar. Granted they're also usually quite packed during lunch and dindin. Ethnic food will usually taste a bit off if you know what it's supposed to taste like. It's not inedible, but obviously not the same as a restaurant. They also have pizza, salad bar, taco bar, and made to order pasta bar every day.

Busch also has a juice bar, Harvest juice bar inside the student center, but they only operate within a short window on weekdays (something like 12-4? Fact check me on this one). One swipe for a smoothie and one more item like a bag of chips.

If you have classes on Busch, or are just towards the back of the campus behind ARC classrooms for some reason, there's Woody's Cafe. They're basically somewhere between a fastfood place and a deli, having mostly burgers, chicken snadwiches, etc. They also have soup, other sides, and you can customize your order to a certain degree. Also good breakfast sandwiches if you're unfortunate enough to be nearby that early in the day.

Livi dining hall is more or less the same as Busch, but I'd say the quality is generally a bit more consistent, albeit they're usually even more crowded. Though they also have panini makers and a variety of ingredients, and a made to order burger bar. Busch used to also have these but they cut access to the panini presses last spring cause folks kept making a mess of it, and they just didn't open the burger bar towards the end. Haven't been there since so idk if any of those are back.

Harvest on Cook Doug is very good. All I have to say about them. Only been twice, had a great experience both times.

Might be a few more places accepting swipes that I forgor or don't know, these are just the ones I've eaten at. So, like I said, plenty of options overall, the food can be good, and is usually at minimum decent.

Edit: grammar, spelling, names (and yes, I meant to spell sandwiches as "snadwiches")
