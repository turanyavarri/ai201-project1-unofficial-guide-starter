Meal Swipe Location Tier List
r/rutgers - Meal Swipe Location Tier List
S-Tier:

Henry's Diner: Truly an elite institution that gives you the true Jersey Diner experience for the cost of only one meal swipe.

Livingston Dining Hall: The best out of all of the dining halls, this place literally has something for every type of eater and more. The burger bar is elite, and the pasta line is my favorite. The pizza bar, asian dishes, and salad bar always have great options too.

Harvest: Amazing food that doesn't make you feel like a bigback. It's delicious, nutritious, and fresh. It's truly hard to beat it.

A-Tier:

Kilmer's: Sandwiches are great, with so many options, including the ability to make a custom sandwich. The fryer station is great, too. Also, a huge selection of snacks and beverages. 10 bucks on a retail swipe here can actually get you a good amount of stuff.

Tres Chiles Truck: I've only gotten this one time, but the food was damn good.

Douglass Cafe: One of the only places at Rutgers with a true made-to-order grill that whips up delicious sandwiches. What sets this place apart is the burrito bar, which gives you an insane amount of food for just one meal swipe. This place is gem.

B-Tier:
Busch Dining Hall: People hate on Busch Dining Hall way toooo much. Honestly, there are a ton of good food options here, and I feel like I have found something good to eat here every single time I have been here.

Woody's: Another great spot for made-to-order food. You can get a good value on a meal swipe. Just not as good as the Douglass Cafe because it has fewer options.

Knight Wagon: Only had this one time year ago because it is never around, but it was pretty good IMO.

Neilson Dining Hall: Another solid dining hall with plenty of good options on any given night. Not my preferred dining hall by any means, but it gets the job done when you are hungry.

C-Tier:

Atrium: The food at the Atrium doesn't taste bad per se, but it is not fit to be the main "dining hall" for College Ave. The food is super oily or processed, and it is super hard to eat here multiple times a week. I gained like 20 pounds from eating here freshman year. Also, the portion sizes here are diabolically huge, so you either end up with leftovers to carry around or you have to throw them out and waste them. You are better off taking the bus to Livi and eating at the dining hall there.

Cafe West: The sandwiches here are honestly kinda mid. They are just reheated, and mine always come out incredibly soggy, even if I eat them right away. The line here is really long at times, so the wait isn't worth it, in my opinion. With that being said you can still get good value out of a cafe west run.

Cook Cafe: Same comments as Cafe West, except no one ever goes here, so at least there is no line.

DC Bagels: This place opened after I stopped getting meal swipes, so I have never been. But considering there used to be a Dunkin where the DC bagels are now, I feel like this automatically has to go to C-tier.

Red Pine Pizza: The pizza here is just okay. That's really all.

D-Tier:

Harvest Juice Bar: This place is a SCAM. One mean swipe worth $14 for one smoothie is crazy work in my opinion. The smoothies are not even healthy, they are just really sugary/sweet. It's an easy way to burn your meal swipes if you have too many.

Sbarro: The pizza is just mid, and the pasta sauce is watery and gross. You are better off just going to the dining hall rather than eating here. The only decent items here are the Stromboli and breadsticks. If you need to take out food, just go to Kilmer's instead. There is no good reason to eat here.

Starbucks Truck: Wasting a whole meal swipe on a cup of coffee and a tiny sandwich or snack, pretty laughable to me. Plus, the line here is always ridiculously long, no matter how hot or cold it is. I am missing something because I genuinely do not understand why people wait in line for this.

These are my reasons for ranking each place. I haven't had a meal plan in years, so some of this might be out of date, but in any case, I'll argue with you in the comments about it.
